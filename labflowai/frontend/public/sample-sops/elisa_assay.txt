ELISA Assay Protocol — Human IL-6 Detection
Version: 2.1
Author: J. Martinez
Date: 2024-11-15

Objective:
Quantify IL-6 concentration in cell culture supernatants using a sandwich ELISA. This protocol is suitable for serum, plasma, and conditioned media samples.

Materials:
- 96-well flat-bottom microplate (high-binding, Nunc MaxiSorp)
- Capture antibody: anti-human IL-6 (R&D Systems, MAB206)
- Detection antibody: biotinylated anti-human IL-6
- Streptavidin-HRP conjugate
- TMB substrate solution
- Stop solution (2N H2SO4)
- Wash buffer: PBS + 0.05% Tween-20
- Blocking buffer: 1% BSA in PBS
- IL-6 recombinant protein standard
- Plate reader capable of reading 450 nm absorbance

Protocol Steps:
1. Coat plate: Dilute capture antibody to 4 µg/mL in PBS. Add 100 µL per well. Seal and incubate overnight at 4°C.

2. Wash: Remove coating solution. Wash wells 3x with wash buffer. Pat dry on paper towel.

3. Block: Add 300 µL blocking buffer per well. Incubate for 1 hour at room temperature.

4. Wash: Wash wells 3x with wash buffer.

5. Add samples and standards: Prepare 8-point standard curve starting from 2000 pg/mL with 2-fold serial dilutions. Add 100 µL sample or standard per well in duplicate. Seal and incubate for 2 hours at room temperature.

6. Wash: Wash wells 5x with wash buffer.

7. Add detection antibody: Dilute biotinylated detection antibody. Add 100 µL per well. Incubate for 1 hour at room temperature.

8. Wash: Wash wells 5x with wash buffer.

9. Add Streptavidin-HRP: Add 100 µL per well. Incubate for 20 minutes at room temperature, protected from light.

10. Wash: Wash wells 5x with wash buffer.

11. TMB substrate: Add 100 µL TMB per well. Incubate for approximately 20 minutes or until standard wells show visible color gradient. Do not allow over-development.

12. Stop reaction: Add 50 µL stop solution per well. Absorbance should shift from blue to yellow.

13. Read plate: Measure absorbance at 450 nm within 30 minutes of stopping reaction. Use 570 nm as reference wavelength if available.

Expected Outputs:
- OD450 readings for each well
- Standard curve (4-parameter logistic fit)
- Calculated IL-6 concentrations in pg/mL
- Coefficient of variation (CV%) for duplicates

Notes:
- All reagents should be equilibrated to room temperature before use.
- Avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles for antibody stocks.
- Samples with OD values outside the standard curve range should be diluted and re-run.
