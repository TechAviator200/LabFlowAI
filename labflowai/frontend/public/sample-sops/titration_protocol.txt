Acid-Base Titration Protocol — Determination of Acetic Acid in Vinegar
Course: Analytical Chemistry Lab
Version: 3.0
Date: 2025-02-20

Objective:
Determine the concentration of acetic acid in commercial vinegar using standardized NaOH solution. Calculate molarity and weight percentage of acetic acid.

Safety:
- Wear gloves and eye protection throughout.
- NaOH is corrosive — handle with care.
- Dispose of waste in labeled neutralization waste container.

Reagents and Equipment:
- Commercial white vinegar sample
- Standardized NaOH solution (approximately 0.1 M — exact concentration from standardization)
- Phenolphthalein indicator solution (1% in ethanol)
- Distilled water
- 50 mL burette (Class A, ±0.05 mL)
- 250 mL Erlenmeyer flasks (x3)
- 10 mL volumetric pipette
- Magnetic stir plate and stir bar
- Analytical balance

Procedure:

1. Burette preparation: Rinse burette with distilled water, then condition with 5 mL NaOH solution. Fill to above the 0.00 mL mark. Allow to drain to exactly 0.00 mL (or record initial volume). Remove air bubbles from tip.

2. Sample preparation: Pipette exactly 10.00 mL of vinegar into a 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask. Add approximately 40 mL distilled water to dilute. Add 3 drops phenolphthalein indicator.

3. Titration: Place flask on magnetic stir plate. Add NaOH from burette dropwise while stirring. As endpoint approaches, add NaOH drop by drop, swirling after each addition.

4. Endpoint detection: Stop titration when a faint persistent pink color remains for at least 30 seconds. Record final burette reading.

5. Calculation:
   - Volume NaOH used = final reading − initial reading
   - Moles NaOH = [NaOH] × volume (L)
   - At equivalence point: moles CH3COOH = moles NaOH
   - Molarity CH3COOH = moles / 0.010 L
   - Weight % = (molarity × MW × 100) / (density × 1000)

6. Repeat titration at least 3 times. Report mean and standard deviation of molarity.

Expected Outputs:
- Initial and final burette readings (mL) for each trial
- Volume of NaOH used (mL) per trial
- Calculated molarity of acetic acid (M)
- Weight percentage of acetic acid (%)
- Mean and standard deviation across trials
- Comparison to labeled vinegar concentration (typically 5% w/v)

Acceptance Criteria:
- Triplicate results within ±0.2 mL of each other
- Calculated % acetic acid within 4–6% for standard commercial vinegar
